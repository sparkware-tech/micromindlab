The Engineer-Educator

Not Just a Teacher.
A Working Professional.

I am an Embedded Systems and IoT Engineer at a Smart Agriculture company, building real-world technology that helps Sri Lankan farmers every single day. From autonomous irrigation systems to crop monitoring sensors — this is what I do professionally.

And now I bring that same industry expertise directly to your home. My classes are not from a textbook. They are from the factory floor, the server rack, and the field.

My passion for innovation started in childhood — taking apart toys, wondering how things work, and dreaming of building something that matters. That curiosity never stopped. I want to light that same spark in every child I teach.

The Learning Path

A Clear Path to the Future

Three progressive stages that take your child from curious beginner to confident innovator.

1
🔦
Stage 1

The Explorer

The foundation of all electronics. Students learn to think like engineers — asking "how does this work?" and building real, working circuits from day one.

  • LED circuits & resistor basics
  • LDR light sensors
  • Ultrasonic distance sensors
  • Breadboard prototyping
  • Block-based visual coding
  • Introduction to Arduino
👦 Ages 8 – 11 · Beginner
2
🤖
Stage 2

The Maker

The exciting leap from circuits to machines. Students build robots that actually move, respond to the environment, and follow their programming commands.

  • DC motors & servo motors
  • Line-following robots
  • Obstacle-avoidance systems
  • Logic-based C++ coding
  • Remote-controlled vehicles
  • Sensor integration
👧 Ages 11 – 14 · Intermediate
3
🌐
Stage 3

The Innovator

Welcome to the Internet of Things — where devices connect to the world. Students build systems that can be monitored and controlled from a smartphone anywhere on earth.

  • ESP32 & Wi-Fi connectivity
  • Blynk & IoT dashboards
  • Mobile app device control
  • Python & web basics
  • Cloud data & automation
  • Smart agriculture projects
🧑 Ages 14 – 17 · Advanced

Project Showcase

Real Projects. Real Learning. Real Pride.

Every project is designed to solve a real-world problem, not just complete an assignment.

🌱 ⚡ Professional Project

Smart Agriculture Monitoring System

Industry Work

A real deployed IoT system using ESP32 microcontrollers and soil moisture, temperature, and humidity sensors to monitor farm conditions 24/7. Data is sent to a cloud dashboard and farmers receive alerts on their mobile phones. This is the kind of innovation students in Stage 3 aspire to build.

ESP32 Soil Moisture Sensor DHT22 MQTT Node-RED Mobile Dashboard
💡 🎓 Student Project

Automatic Night Light

Using an LDR (light-dependent resistor), students built a circuit that automatically turns on an LED when the room gets dark — no button needed. Concept: energy conservation and smart automation.

Arduino LDR Sensor LED Circuit C++ Code
🤖 🎓 Student Project

Obstacle-Avoiding Robot

A mobile robot built with Arduino, DC motors, and an ultrasonic sensor. The robot detects walls and objects and autonomously steers away — just like a self-driving car, at pocket size.

Arduino HC-SR04 L298N Motor Driver DC Motors
🇱🇰 🌾 Sri Lanka Innovation

Smart Water Pump for Sri Lankan Farmers

Stage 3 Goal Project

This is the capstone goal for Stage 3 students: build an automated irrigation pump controlled by a mobile phone. Using an ESP32, a soil moisture sensor, and a relay module, students create a system that checks if the soil is dry and activates a water pump automatically — or via WhatsApp. Technology that directly helps Sri Lankan agriculture. Technology that matters.
